Expert Advice

The Complete Magnetic Screen Buying Guide

Choosing the right magnetic insect screen can feel overwhelming when you're faced with size options, mesh grades, and closure types. This guide walks you through everything you need to know so you can buy with confidence — whether you're protecting a single door or your entire home.

Understanding Magnetic Window Screens

Magnetic window screens use a fine polyester or fibreglass mesh supported by a simple frame or mounting tape, with neodymium magnets sewn into the centre seam (for door screens) or along the edges (for window screens). The magnets ensure the screen seals tightly against the frame or closes automatically after use, making them the most convenient type of insect screen available.

Unlike traditional framed screens, magnetic screens don't require special tools, permanent fixtures, or professional installation. This makes them ideal for renters, people who move frequently, or anyone who wants a seasonal solution they can put up and take down with ease.

Choosing the Right Size

Getting the size right is the single most important step. Measure the full opening — not the door leaf or window pane, but the frame-to-frame dimension including the rebate. For door screens, you generally want the screen to overlap the door frame by at least 2–3cm on each side. This ensures a tight seal with no gaps for insects to sneak through.

Standard Door Sizes

Most exterior doors fall within standard width ranges: 80cm, 90cm, and 100cm are the most common, with heights typically between 200cm and 220cm. Double doors may be 160cm to 200cm wide. If your door falls within a standard size, ordering is simple. If not, look for a custom-size option or contact the store directly.

Window Sizing Tips

For window screens, measure the inside of the window frame. Casement windows that open outward are best suited to magnetic screens mounted on the inside face of the frame. Sash windows that slide up and down work well too, particularly if the upper sash is kept closed and only the lower sash opened for ventilation.

Mesh Quality and Insect Protection

Not all mesh is equal. The aperture size (the tiny gap in the weave) determines which insects are blocked. A standard aperture of around 1.2mm will stop mosquitoes, flies, moths, gnats and most other flying insects. Screens with a slightly coarser weave may allow very small insects through — so check the mesh specification before buying.

Fibreglass mesh is durable, UV-resistant and less prone to denting than aluminium alternatives. Polyester mesh is lighter and more flexible, making it ideal for magnetic screens that need to hang freely and move as you walk through. For the highest level of protection, look for screens labelled as suitable for mosquito or midge exclusion, which typically indicates the tightest mesh available.

Magnetic Closure Strength

The quality of the magnets matters enormously for long-term performance. Neodymium magnets (also called rare-earth magnets) are significantly stronger than standard ferrite magnets for their size, meaning fewer magnets are needed and the seam holds more reliably. A well-designed door screen will have 18 or more magnets spaced evenly along the seam to ensure closure from top to bottom without gaps.

Self-Aligning Magnet Design

The best magnetic screens use alternating polarity placement so the magnets actively pull the seam into alignment even if the screen moves off-centre as you walk through. This is an important feature in busy households where children and pets frequently push through the screen quickly and at odd angles.

Installation Methods

Most magnetic screens mount using adhesive hook-and-loop tape, which bonds firmly to clean, dry frames and peels away cleanly when removed. Some screens also include thumbtacks for extra security on timber frames. When installing, always clean the frame surface thoroughly with a dry cloth before applying the adhesive. Press the tape firmly and allow it to cure for an hour before hanging the screen.

Tips for a Better Installation

  • Run the tape continuously around the full perimeter — gaps in the tape create gaps in the seal.
  • Start at the top of the frame and work downward to keep the screen square.
  • For very wide doors, a second person makes installation much easier.
  • If your frame has an unusual surface (rough stone, textured paint, or aluminium), test a small piece of tape first to check adhesion.

Screens for Specific Use Cases

High-Traffic Family Homes

In homes with multiple children and pets coming and going throughout the day, durability and ease of use are priorities. Look for screens with reinforced mesh edges, strong neodymium magnets, and robust hook-and-loop tape. Magnetic door screens are ideal because children and dogs can push straight through without slowing down — and the screen closes itself reliably every time.

Renters and Temporary Accommodation

Magnetic screens are the perfect insect solution for renters because they require no permanent alterations to the property. The adhesive tape holds firmly during use but peels away without damage when you move out. They're lightweight enough to pack with your belongings and reinstall in a new home quickly.

Caravans and Holiday Homes

Magnetic screens are extremely popular in the caravan and holiday home market. They're compact to store, fast to install and remove, and work on both doors and windows. If you're spending time in areas with high mosquito or fly pressure — coastal locations, near rivers, tropical climates — a magnetic screen makes a significant difference to your comfort.

Patio and Veranda Openings

Wide patio openings can be covered using two screens side by side, or by ordering an extra-wide screen sized to the full opening. Double-door magnetic screens are a popular choice for patio doors, allowing free movement while keeping the outdoor area connected to the indoor space without letting insects in.

Care and Maintenance

A well-maintained magnetic screen will last for multiple seasons. Light maintenance is straightforward: shake or brush the mesh to remove dust and debris, and wipe the frame and tape border with a damp cloth. For deeper cleaning, detach the screen and hand wash in cool water. Always allow it to dry completely before reattaching to prevent mildew.

At the end of summer or insect season, remove the screen, fold it flat, and store it in a cool, dry place. The hook-and-loop tape on the frame can be left in place ready for next season. Replacing only the tape every few years is far cheaper than buying a new screen and keeps the installation fresh and secure.

Why Magnetic Screens Outperform Other Options

Compared to plug-in insect killers, chemical sprays, coils, and candles, magnetic insect screens provide continuous protection without any ongoing cost, odour, or health concerns. Once installed, they simply work — 24 hours a day, without batteries, chemicals, or maintenance. For households that want clean air flowing freely without the insect problem, a magnetic screen is consistently the most practical and cost-effective solution.
