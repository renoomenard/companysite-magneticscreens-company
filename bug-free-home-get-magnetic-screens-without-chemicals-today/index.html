What Are Magnetic Screens and How Do They Work

Think of magnetic screens as nature's own doorman – they know exactly who to let in and who to keep out. These ingenious devices consist of fine mesh material embedded with powerful magnets along the edges. When installed on windows, doors, or other openings, they create an automatic closure system that opens when you pass through and seals shut immediately afterward.

The magic happens in the magnetic strips. These aren't your ordinary refrigerator magnets – they're specially designed with precise polarity to ensure a strong, reliable seal every time. The mesh itself is crafted from durable materials that can withstand daily use while maintaining excellent visibility and airflow.

Myth: Magnetic Screens Don't Work in Windy Conditions

Quality magnetic screens are specifically designed to handle normal wind conditions. The magnetic force is calibrated to maintain closure against typical breezes while still allowing easy human passage. Extreme weather conditions might require temporary screen removal, but normal windy days pose no problems.

Myth: Magnetic Screens Interfere with Electronics

Modern magnetic screens use localized magnetic fields that don't extend beyond the immediate closure area. They won't affect televisions, computers, or other electronic devices in your home. The magnetic strength is specifically calibrated for screen closure, not electronic interference.
